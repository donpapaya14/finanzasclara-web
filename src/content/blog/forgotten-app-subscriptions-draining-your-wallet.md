## Introduction to Forgotten App Subscriptions
The average user wastes **$216 per year** on unused app subscriptions, according to a 2023 study by [C+R Research](https://www.crresearch.com). This staggering figure highlights the need to regularly review and **cancel forgotten app subscriptions**. Streaming services and productivity tools are among the top offenders, with many users unaware of the **hidden subscription costs**.

## iOS: Find and Cancel Subscriptions in 90 Seconds
To cancel subscriptions on iOS, follow these steps:
1. Open the **Settings** app.
2. Tap on **[Your Name]**.
3. Select **Subscriptions**.
4. Review your active subscriptions and **cancel** any unused ones.
According to [Apple Support Documentation](https://support.apple.com), you can also view expired subscriptions that might auto-renew by tapping on **Expired**.

## Android's Hidden Subscription Manager
To manage subscriptions on Android, follow these steps:
1. Open the **Google Play Store**.
2. Tap on your **Profile**.
3. Select **Payments & subscriptions**.
4. Review your active subscriptions and **cancel** any unused ones.
Note that carrier-billed subscriptions may not appear in the Google Play Store, as stated by [Google Play Help](https://support.google.com/googleplay).

## The 5-Minute Subscription Audit System
To conduct a subscription audit, follow these steps:
1. Review your bank statements for the past 3 months.
2. Look for transaction codes like **APL*** or **GOOGLE***.
3. Identify any recurring charges for unused subscriptions.
4. Set a calendar reminder to review your subscriptions every 3 months.
According to the [National Foundation for Credit Counseling](https://www.nfcc.org), this simple system can help you avoid **hidden subscription costs**.
