## Introduction to Monthly Budget Templates for High Income Earners
For individuals earning **$75,000** per year, creating a **monthly budget template** is crucial for effective **money management** and achieving long-term financial goals. According to [The Financial Diet](https://thefinancialdiet.com), a well-structured budget helps in allocating resources efficiently. This article provides a comprehensive guide on how to create a **monthly budget template for high income earners**, focusing on the **70/20/10 budgeting rule**.

## Understanding the 70/20/10 Budgeting Rule
The **70/20/10 budgeting rule** suggests allocating **70%** of the income towards essential expenses, **20%** towards savings and debt repayment, and **10%** towards discretionary spending. As per [The Financial Diet (2020)](https://thefinancialdiet.com), this rule helps in maintaining a balance between necessary expenses, savings, and leisure activities.

## Essential Expenses (50% of $75,000 income)
Essential expenses include housing costs, transportation expenses, food and dining, and insurance premiums. According to the [US Bureau of Labor Statistics (2022)](https://www.bls.gov), the average American spends around **30%** of their income on housing. Here are some examples of essential expenses that make up **50%** of the budget:
* Housing costs: **$1,875** per month (based on a **$2,250** monthly mortgage payment)
* Transportation expenses: **$500** per month (including car loan, insurance, and fuel)
* Food and dining: **$750** per month (for groceries and dining out)
* Insurance premiums: **$200** per month (health, life, and disability insurance)

## Savings and Debt Repayment (20% of $75,000 income)
Savings and debt repayment are crucial aspects of personal finance planning. As per the [National Foundation for Credit Counseling (2020)](https://www.nfcc.org), allocating **20%** of the income towards savings and debt repayment helps in building a secure financial future. Here are some strategies for allocating **20%** of the budget:
1. Emergency fund contributions: **$1,250** per month (aiming for 3-6 months' worth of expenses)
2. Retirement savings: **$500** per month (through a 401(k) or IRA)
3. High-interest debt payoff: **$250** per month (credit cards, personal loans, etc.)

## Discretionary Spending (10% of $75,000 income)
Discretionary spending includes entertainment expenses, travel, and hobbies. According to the [Bureau of Labor Statistics (2022)](https://www.bls.gov), the average American spends around **5%** of their income on entertainment. Here are some ideas for discretionary spending that make up **10%** of the budget:
* Entertainment expenses: **$375** per month (dining out, movies, etc.)
* Travel: **$250** per month (vacations, weekend getaways, etc.)
* Hobbies: **$125** per month (pursuing interests and passions)

Common budgeting mistakes include underestimating expenses, overestimating income, and neglecting emergency fund contributions. According to [Kiplinger (2020)](https://www.kiplinger.com), avoiding these mistakes can help create a more accurate and effective budget plan.
