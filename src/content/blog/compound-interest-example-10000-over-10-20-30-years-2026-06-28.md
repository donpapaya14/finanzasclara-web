## The $10,000 Test: 3 Growth Scenarios
Investing $10,000 with **compound interest** can significantly impact your long-term wealth. Using the [SEC Compound Interest Calculator](https://www.sec.gov), we can see the growth of $10,000 over 10, 20, and 30 years at 5%, 7%, and 10% annual returns.

| Years | 5% Return | 7% Return | 10% Return |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 10 | $16,289 | $19,672 | $25,937 |
| 20 | $33,959 | $49,323 | $82,864 |
| 30 | $43,219 | $76,123 | $174,494 |

## Why the 7% Benchmark Matters
The **7% benchmark** is often used as it approximates the historical **S&P 500 returns** (adjusted for inflation) from 1950-2023, according to the [NYU Stern Historical Returns Dataset](https://www.stern.nyu.edu). This dataset shows that over the long term, the S&P 500 has provided returns of around 7% per year.

## The Shocking 30-Year Difference
The difference in growth between a 5% and 10% return over 30 years is staggering. Starting with $10,000, the investment grows to **$43,219** at 5% and **$174,494** at 10%. This highlights the importance of **long-term investing growth** and the power of compound interest.

## Monthly Contributions Supercharge Growth
Adding **$100/month** to the initial $10,000 investment at a 7% return nearly triples the 30-year returns, from **$76,123** to **$209,761**, as shown by the [Investor.gov Compound Interest Tool](https://www.investor.gov). This demonstrates the impact of consistent investing.

## Frequently Asked Questions
### What is compound interest?
Compound interest is the interest calculated on the initial principal, which also includes all the accumulated interest from previous periods. According to the [Federal Reserve](https://www.federalreserve.gov), it's a key factor in **long-term investing growth**.

### How does compound interest work?
Compound interest works by adding the interest to the principal at regular intervals, so that the interest in the next period is calculated on the new principal balance. The [SEC](https://www.sec.gov) provides tools to calculate compound interest.

### What are the benefits of compound interest?
The benefits of compound interest include the potential for significant growth over time, the ability to earn interest on interest, and the power to build wealth. As discussed in [The Simple Path to Wealth by JL Collins](https://www.amazon.com), compound interest is a key component of **investing**.

### Can I use compound interest to my advantage?
Yes, you can use compound interest to your advantage by starting to invest early, being consistent with your investments, and taking advantage of high-yield savings accounts or investments. The [Consumer Financial Protection Bureau](https://www.consumerfinance.gov) provides resources on how to make the most of compound interest.

### How do I calculate compound interest?
You can calculate compound interest using a formula or a compound interest calculator, such as the one provided by the [SEC](https://www.sec.gov). The formula is A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt), where A is the amount of money accumulated after n years, including interest, P is the principal amount (the initial amount of money), r is the annual interest rate (in decimal), n is the number of times that interest is compounded per year, and t is the time the money is invested for in years.
