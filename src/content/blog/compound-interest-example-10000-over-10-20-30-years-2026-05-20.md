## The $10,000 Test: 3 Growth Scenarios
To illustrate the power of **compound interest**, let's consider a $10,000 investment at 5%, 7%, and 9% annual returns over 10, 20, and 30 years. According to the [SEC Compound Interest Calculator](https://www.sec.gov), the results are as follows:
| Years | 5% Return | 7% Return | 9% Return |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 10 | $16,288.95 | $19,672.17 | $23,673.64 |
| 20 | $33,065.92 | $49,323.32 | $74,281.48 |
| 30 | $55,368.47 | $94,462.57 | $162,488.32 |
The difference between a 5% and 9% return is substantial, with the latter resulting in **almost 3 times** the amount after 30 years.

## The 7-Year Doubling Rule (Why Time Beats Timing)
The **Rule of 72** states that to find the number of years it takes for an investment to double, you divide 72 by the annual return percentage. For example, at a 10% return, the investment doubles every **7.2 years**, while at 7%, it takes **10.3 years**. As shown by the [Federal Reserve Economic Data](https://fred.stlouisfed.org), this highlights the importance of long-term investing. A visual timeline graphic illustrates this concept:
* 10% return: $10,000 -> $20,000 in 7.2 years -> $40,000 in 14.4 years
* 7% return: $10,000 -> $20,000 in 10.3 years -> $40,000 in 20.6 years

## Frequently Asked Questions
### What is compound interest?
Compound interest is the interest calculated on the initial principal and also on the accumulated interest from previous periods. According to the [SEC](https://www.sec.gov), it can help investments grow exponentially over time.
### How does compound interest work?
Compound interest works by adding the interest earned in each period to the principal, so that the interest earned in the next period is calculated on the new, higher principal. For example, if you invest $10,000 at a 5% annual return, you will earn $500 in interest in the first year, making the principal $10,500.
### What is the Rule of 72?
The Rule of 72 is a formula for estimating how long it will take for an investment to double in value based on the interest rate it earns. According to the [Federal Reserve Economic Data](https://fred.stlouisfed.org), it is calculated by dividing 72 by the annual return percentage.
### How can I calculate compound interest?
You can calculate compound interest using a formula or a calculator. The formula is A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt), where A is the amount of money accumulated after n years, including interest, P is the principal amount, r is the annual interest rate (in decimal), n is the number of times that interest is compounded per year, and t is the time the money is invested for in years.
### What are the benefits of compound interest?
The benefits of compound interest include the potential for exponential growth, the ability to earn interest on interest, and the power to build wealth over time. According to the [Investor.gov](https://www.investor.gov), it can help investors achieve their long-term financial goals.
### How can I maximize the benefits of compound interest?
To maximize the benefits of compound interest, consider investing for the long term, taking advantage of dollar-cost averaging, and minimizing expense ratios. According to the [Vanguard](https://www.vanguard.com), it is also important to start early and be consistent with your investments.
