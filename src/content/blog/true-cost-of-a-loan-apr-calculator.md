## What is APR and how is it calculated?
The **Annual Percentage Rate (APR)** is the interest rate charged on a loan or credit product over a year, including fees. According to the [Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED)](https://fred.stlouisfed.org), APR is calculated using the formula: APR = (Total interest paid / Total amount borrowed) * (Number of periods / Number of payments per period). For example, to calculate the APR for a $10,000 loan with a 5-year repayment period and a monthly payment of $193, we can use the following steps:
1. Determine the total interest paid over the life of the loan.
2. Calculate the total amount borrowed.
3. Determine the number of periods and the number of payments per period.
Using this formula, we can calculate the APR for the $10,000 loan: APR = (Total interest paid / $10,000) * (12 / 12) = **6.17%**.

## How APR Affects Loan Costs Over Time
According to [Investopedia's article on compound interest](https://www.investopedia.com), the APR affects loan costs over time due to compounding interest. The following table illustrates the impact of APR on loan costs:
| APR | Total Interest Paid | Total Cost of Borrowing |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 5% | $2,645 | $12,645 |
| 6% | $3,347 | $13,347 |
| 7% | $4,183 | $14,183 |
As shown in the table, a higher APR results in a higher total interest paid and total cost of borrowing.

## Tips for Comparing Loan Offers and Choosing the Best APR
When comparing loan offers, it's essential to read loan terms carefully and consider the following factors:
1. **APR**: The lower the APR, the less you'll pay in interest over the life of the loan.
2. **Fees**: Look for loans with minimal or no fees.
3. **Repayment term**: A longer repayment term may result in lower monthly payments, but you'll pay more in interest over the life of the loan.
According to the [Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB)](https://www.consumerfinance.gov), borrowers should also consider the following checklist when choosing a loan:
* **Loan amount**: Ensure the loan amount meets your needs.
* **Interest rate**: Consider the interest rate and how it affects the APR.
* **Fees**: Look for loans with minimal or no fees.

## Common APR Myths and Misconceptions Debunked
According to [Kiplinger's article on APR myths](https://www.kiplinger.com), there are several common myths and misconceptions about APR. One of the most significant myths is that APR and interest rate are the same thing. However, APR includes fees, while interest rate does not. Another myth is that a lower APR always means a better loan. However, borrowers should consider other factors, such as fees and repayment terms, when choosing a loan.
