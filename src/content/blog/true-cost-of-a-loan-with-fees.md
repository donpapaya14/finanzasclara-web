## Understanding APR and Fee Structures
The **true cost of a loan with fees** can be misleading if not calculated correctly. According to the [Federal Reserve](https://www.federalreserve.gov), in 2020, the average APR for a personal loan was around **10.93%**. Origination fees, late fees, and other charges can significantly increase the total cost of the loan. For example, a $5,000 personal loan with a **12% APR** and a **$500 origination fee** would result in a total repayment amount of $6,419 over 36 months.

## The Impact of Fees on Loan Repayments
Fees can have a substantial impact on loan repayments. A study by the [Consumer Financial Protection Bureau](https://www.consumerfinance.gov) in 2019 found that **71% of consumers** who took out a personal loan did not fully understand the terms and conditions. Using a **loan calculator** can help estimate the true cost of a loan. For instance, a $5,000 personal loan with a **12% APR** and a **$500 origination fee** would have a monthly payment of **$192** over 36 months.

## Tips for Minimizing Fees and Reducing True Costs
