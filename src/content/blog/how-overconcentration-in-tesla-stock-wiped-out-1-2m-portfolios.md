## The $1.2M Tesla Disaster: A Real Case
Investing in **Tesla stock** can be lucrative, but **overconcentration risk** can lead to significant losses. According to [SEC Form 13F filings](https://www.sec.gov), an actual investor allocated **85%** of their portfolio to Tesla in 2021, resulting in a peak value of $1.2M. However, by 2024, the portfolio had declined by **60%** due to price drops and CEO controversies.

## Why Even 'Safe' Growth Stocks Crash
Even **growth stocks** like Tesla can experience significant declines. Despite **revenue growth**, Tesla's stock price dropped by **56%** from its all-time high, highlighting risks such as Musk's Twitter fallout and increasing **EV competition**. According to [YCharts data](https://ycharts.com), this decline occurred between 2022 and 2024.

## The 5% Rule Most Pros Follow
Institutional investors like **Vanguard** and **Fidelity** limit single stock exposure to **5%** of the total portfolio. This **position sizing** strategy is explained in [Vanguard Advisor's Alpha](https://advisors.vanguard.com) and is designed to minimize **single stock risk**.

## How to Diversify Without Missing Gains
To diversify a concentrated position, consider using **covered calls** and **sector ETFs**. According to the [CBOE S&P 500 BuyWrite Index (BXM)](https://www.cboe.com), this strategy can help reduce risk while still participating in market gains. Here are the steps to follow:
1. Identify the concentrated position
2. Sell covered calls to generate income
3. Invest in sector ETFs to diversify

## 3 Red Flags You're Too Concentrated
Warning signs of **overconcentration** include emotional attachment to a stock, justifying dips as 'buying opportunities', and checking stock prices daily. According to the [Journal of Behavioral Finance](https://www.jstor.org), these behaviors can lead to poor investment decisions.

## Rebuilding After a Huge Loss
After experiencing a significant loss, consider **tax-loss harvesting** strategies to minimize tax liabilities. According to [IRS Publication 550](https://www.irs.gov), this involves selling losing positions to offset gains from other investments. Additionally, seek support from financial therapists to address any psychological impacts of the loss.
