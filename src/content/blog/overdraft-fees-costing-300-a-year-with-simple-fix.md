## Introduction to Overdraft Fees Prevention Savings
Overdraft fees can cost individuals around **$300 a year**, with the average fee ranging from **$25 to $35** per transaction, according to a [Federal Reserve](https://www.federalreserve.gov) 2022 study on overdraft fees. To avoid these fees, it's essential to understand how overdraft fees work and implement **overdraft fees prevention savings** strategies.

## How Overdraft Fees Work
Banks charge overdraft fees when an account holder's balance falls below zero. The average overdraft fee is **$29.80**, with some banks charging as high as **$40** per transaction, as reported by a [Bankrate](https://www.bankrate.com) 2022 survey on overdraft fees. To avoid these fees, individuals can set up **overdraft protection**, which transfers funds from a linked account or credit card to cover overdrafts.

## The Simple Fix: Setting Up Overdraft Protection
To set up overdraft protection, follow these steps:
1. Log in to your online banking account or visit a bank branch.
2. Select the account you want to protect.
3. Choose the overdraft protection option, such as a linked savings or credit card.
4. Set up the transfer amount and frequency.
As recommended by the [Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB)](https://www.consumerfinance.gov), setting up overdraft protection can help avoid **overdraft fees** and save individuals around **$300 a year**.

Common mistakes that lead to overdraft fees include:
