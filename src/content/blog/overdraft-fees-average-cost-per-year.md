## Understanding Overdraft Fees
The **average overdraft fee** is around $35 per transaction, according to the [Consumer Financial Protection Bureau](https://www.consumerfinance.gov). This fee is typically triggered when an account holder's balance falls below zero. The [Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (2020)](https://www.consumerfinance.gov) reports that overdraft fees are often a result of debit card transactions, ATM withdrawals, or checks.

## Average Overdraft Fee Costs Per Year
The **average overdraft fee cost per year** varies by age group and income level. According to [Bankrate's 2022 Overdraft Fee Study](https://www.bankrate.com), the average overdraft fee cost per year is around $300. Here is a breakdown of the average overdraft fee costs per year by age group and income level:
| Age Group | Income Level | Average Overdraft Fee Cost Per Year |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 18-24 | Low | $250 |
| 25-34 | Medium | $300 |
| 35-44 | High | $350 |
| 45-54 | Low | $200 |
| 55-64 | Medium | $250 |
| 65+ | High | $300 |

## Common Mistakes That Lead to Overdraft Fees
To avoid overdraft fees, it's essential to monitor your account balance regularly. Here are some practical tips to help you avoid overdraft fees:
1. **Set up overdraft protection**: Link a savings account or credit card to your checking account to cover overdrafts.
2. **Monitor your account balance**: Check your account balance regularly to avoid overdrafts.
3. **Use an overdraft fee calculator**: Calculate your average annual overdraft fee cost using an [overdraft fee calculator](https://www.nerdwallet.com).
