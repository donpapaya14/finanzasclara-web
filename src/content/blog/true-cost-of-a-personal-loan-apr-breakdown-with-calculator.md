## Introduction to the True Cost of Personal Loans
The **true cost of a personal loan** is often misunderstood, with many borrowers focusing solely on the interest rate. However, the Annual Percentage Rate (APR) provides a more comprehensive picture, including origination fees and other costs. According to the [Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB)](https://www.consumerfinance.gov), APR is a crucial factor in determining the total cost of a loan.

## What APR and Why It Matters More Than Interest Rate
APR is defined as the annual rate charged for borrowing, including fees. For instance, a loan with a 5% interest rate may have a 7% APR when considering origination fees. This distinction is vital, as it significantly impacts the total repayment amount. The [Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB)](https://www.consumerfinance.gov) 2023 guidelines emphasize the importance of understanding APR.

## 3 Hidden Fees That Inflate Your Loan Cost
Several fees can increase the cost of a personal loan, including:
* Origination fees (1-6%): A $10,000 loan with a 5% origination fee adds $500 to the total cost.
* Prepayment penalties: Charged when a borrower pays off the loan early.
* Late payment fees: Applied when monthly payments are missed or delayed. The [Federal Reserve Bank of New York](https://www.newyorkfed.org) 2022 report highlights these common fees.

To lower the APR, consider the following:

## Frequently Asked Questions
### What is the average APR for a personal loan?
The average APR for a personal loan varies depending on credit score and lender. According to [Experian](https://www.experian.com), the average APR for a personal loan is around 10.46%.
### How do I calculate the total cost of a personal loan?
To calculate the total cost, use an online loan calculator or consider the principal, interest, and fees. The [Texas Instruments BA II Plus Professional Financial Calculator](https://www.amazon.com/Texas-Instruments-BA-II-Plus) can also be used.
### What are the benefits of a shorter loan term?
A shorter loan term results in lower total interest paid and faster debt repayment. However, monthly payments may be higher.
### Can I negotiate the APR with my lender?
Yes, negotiating the APR with your lender is possible, especially if you have a good credit score. Consider asking for fee waivers or opting for autopay discounts.
### How does credit score affect personal loan APR?
A good credit score can result in a lower APR, while a poor credit score may lead to a higher APR. According to [FICO](https://www.fico.com), a credit score above 700 is considered good.
### What are the consequences of missing a loan payment?
Missing a loan payment can result in late payment fees, damage to your credit score, and potential collections. It is essential to make timely payments to avoid these consequences.
