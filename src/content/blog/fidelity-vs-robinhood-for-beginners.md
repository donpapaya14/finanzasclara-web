## Introduction to Fidelity and Robinhood

Fidelity and Robinhood are two popular investment platforms that cater to different needs of investors. Fidelity, founded in 1946, is a well-established financial services company with a wide range of investment products and services. Robinhood, launched in 2013, is a mobile-first brokerage firm that offers commission-free trading and a user-friendly interface. While both platforms aim to make investing accessible to everyone, they have distinct features and target different types of investors.

According to a 2024 survey by Fidelity Investments, 71% of their clients are first-time investors, and 62% of Robinhood users are under the age of 35 [1]. This indicates that both platforms are popular among beginners and younger investors.

### Fees and Commissions Comparison

Fidelity and Robinhood have different fee structures for various investment products. Fidelity charges $0 commission for online stock, ETF, and options trades, while Robinhood offers commission-free trading for stocks, ETFs, options, and cryptocurrencies. However, Fidelity has higher fees for mutual funds and other investment products.

### Account Minimums and Promotions

Fidelity and Robinhood have different account minimums and promotional offers for new customers. Fidelity has a minimum account balance requirement of $2,500 for certain investment products, while Robinhood does not have any account minimums.

| Account Minimum | Fidelity | Robinhood |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Stocks | **$2,500** | **$0** |
| ETFs | **$2,500** | **$0** |
| Options | **$2,500** | **$0** |
| Mutual Funds | **$2,500** | **$0** |

Robinhood does not have any account minimums, making it a more accessible option for investors with smaller account balances. However, Fidelity's promotional offers, such as the $100 bonus for new customers, make it a more attractive option for investors who are willing to meet the account minimum requirements.
