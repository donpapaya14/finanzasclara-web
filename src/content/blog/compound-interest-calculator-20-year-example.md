## The Formula Behind Compound Interest
The compound interest formula is A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt), where A is the amount of money accumulated after n years, including interest, P is the principal amount (initial investment), r is the annual interest rate (in decimal), n is the number of times that interest is compounded per year, and t is the time the money is invested for in years. According to Investopedia's article on compound interest formula, this formula can help you calculate the future value of your investment.
