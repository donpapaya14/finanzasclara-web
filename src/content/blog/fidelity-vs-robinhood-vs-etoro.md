## Introduction to Free Brokers

For beginners seeking the **best free brokers**, Fidelity, Robinhood, and eToro stand out as top contenders. Fidelity, established in 1946, offers a comprehensive suite of investment products with zero-commission trading. Robinhood, founded in 2013, revolutionized the industry with its mobile-first approach and commission-free trades. eToro, launched in 2007, specializes in social trading and cryptocurrency. According to [Investopedia (2022)](https://www.investopedia.com), these platforms cater to different investor profiles: Fidelity for long-term investors, Robinhood for active traders, and eToro for those interested in copy trading.

## Fees Comparison

[NerdWallet (2023)](https://www.nerdwallet.com) notes that while all three offer commission-free stock trading, eToro charges a **$5 withdrawal fee** and higher spreads on cryptocurrencies. Fidelity’s $0.65 per options contract is competitive for advanced traders.

## Investment Options

Each broker offers distinct **investment products**:

1. **Fidelity**: Stocks, ETFs, mutual funds, options, bonds, and fractional shares. No direct cryptocurrency trading.
2. **Robinhood**: Stocks, ETFs, options, cryptocurrencies, and fractional shares. No mutual funds.
3. **eToro**: Stocks, ETFs, cryptocurrencies, and CFDs. Unique "CopyTrader" feature for mimicking top investors.

[The Balance (2022)](https://www.thebalance.com) highlights eToro’s **social trading** as a standout for beginners, while Fidelity’s mutual fund selection appeals to retirement savers.

## Mobile App and Platform Review

User experience varies significantly:

- **Fidelity**: Robust desktop platform (Active Trader Pro) with a functional mobile app. Best for research-heavy users.
- **Robinhood**: Sleek, intuitive mobile app with limited desktop functionality. Ideal for casual traders.
- **eToro**: Social-focused interface with performance tracking. Mobile app scores 4.5/5 on [Forbes (2023)](https://www.forbes.com).

## Customer Support and Education

Educational resources differ:

1. **Fidelity**: Extensive webinars, articles, and 24/7 phone support.
2. **Robinhood**: Basic FAQs and email support. No phone support.
3. **eToro**: Video tutorials and a virtual portfolio for practice.

[Kiplinger (2022)](https://www.kiplinger.com) ranks Fidelity highest for **customer support**, while eToro leads in community-driven learning.

## Conclusion and Recommendation

For **best free brokers for beginners**:
- Choose **Fidelity** for long-term investing and research tools.
- Opt for **Robinhood** if you prefer a simple, mobile-first experience.
- Pick **eToro** for cryptocurrency and social trading features.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Which broker is best for beginners with no fees?
**Robinhood and eToro offer zero-commission trading**, but Fidelity provides more educational resources. For absolute beginners, Fidelity’s support system is invaluable.

### Does eToro charge fees for stock trading?
**eToro offers commission-free stock trading** but charges a 1% spread on cryptocurrencies and a $5 withdrawal fee.

### Can I trade options on Robinhood?
**Yes, Robinhood allows options trading** with no per-contract fees, making it cost-effective for frequent traders.

### Is Fidelity good for cryptocurrency trading?
**Fidelity does not support direct cryptocurrency trading**. For crypto, eToro or Robinhood are better options.

### Which broker has the best mobile app?
**Robinhood’s app is the most user-friendly**, according to [Forbes (2023)](https://www.forbes.com), with a 4.8/5 rating for ease of use.

## My Take

As an app developer and trader, I’ve tested all three platforms extensively. **Robinhood’s simplicity** is perfect for quick trades, but its lack of customer support can be frustrating. Fidelity’s depth is impressive, but the interface feels outdated. eToro’s social features are fun, but the withdrawal fees are a downside. For beginners, I’d recommend starting with **Fidelity** for its reliability, then exploring Robinhood or eToro as you gain confidence.
