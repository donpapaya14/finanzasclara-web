## Introduction to Micro Investing
Micro investing apps are a type of **investment platform** that allows users to invest small amounts of money into a diversified portfolio. According to [Investopedia](https://www.investopedia.com), micro investing has become increasingly popular in recent years, with **over 10 million users** in the United States alone. The benefits of micro investing include **low risk investments**, **passive income**, and the ability to invest with as little as $1.

## Top Micro Investing Apps
Some of the most popular micro investing apps include:
* Acorns: With **over 8 million users**, Acorns is one of the largest micro investing apps. It offers a range of investment portfolios and has a **$1 monthly fee**.
* Stash: Stash allows users to invest as little as $5 and has a **$1 monthly fee**. It also offers a range of investment portfolios and **educational resources**.
* Clink: Clink allows users to invest as little as $1 and has a **$1 monthly fee**. It also offers a range of investment portfolios and **retirement accounts**.

## Returns and Risks of Micro Investing
The average returns of micro investing apps vary depending on the portfolio and market conditions. According to [The Balance](https://www.thebalance.com), the average annual return of micro investing apps is around **4-6%**. However, it's essential to note that micro investing apps also come with **risks**, such as market volatility and **fees**.

## Getting Started with Micro Investing
To get started with micro investing, follow these steps:
1. **Download a micro investing app**, such as Acorns or Stash.
2. **Create an account** and provide some basic information, such as your name and address.
3. **Fund your account** with a minimum of $1.
4. **Choose a portfolio** that aligns with your investment goals and risk tolerance.
5. **Monitor and adjust** your portfolio as needed.

## Tax Implications of Micro Investing
The tax implications of micro investing vary depending on the type of account and investment. According to [TurboTax](https://www.turbotax.com), micro investing apps are subject to **capital gains tax**, which can range from **10-20%**. However, some micro investing apps also offer **tax-advantaged accounts**, such as Roth IRAs.

## Frequently Asked Questions
### What is micro investing?
Micro investing is a type of investment platform that allows users to invest small amounts of money into a diversified portfolio. According to [NerdWallet](https://www.nerdwallet.com), micro investing has become increasingly popular in recent years, with **over 10 million users** in the United States alone.
### How does micro investing work?
Micro investing works by allowing users to invest small amounts of money into a diversified portfolio. The portfolio is typically managed by a team of experts, and the user can monitor and adjust their portfolio as needed.
### What are the benefits of micro investing?
The benefits of micro investing include **low risk investments**, **passive income**, and the ability to invest with as little as $1. According to [Forbes](https://www.forbes.com), micro investing has become a popular way for millennials to invest in the stock market.
### What are the risks of micro investing?
The risks of micro investing include **market volatility** and **fees**. According to [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com), micro investing apps also come with **regulatory risks**, such as changes in government regulations.
### Can I use micro investing for retirement?
Yes, some micro investing apps offer **retirement accounts**, such as Roth IRAs. According to [The Balance](https://www.thebalance.com), micro investing can be a great way to save for retirement, especially for those who are just starting out.
### How do I choose a micro investing app?
To choose a micro investing app, consider the following factors:
* **Fees**: Look for apps with low or no fees.
* **Minimum investment**: Consider apps with low or no minimum investment requirements.
* **Portfolio options**: Look for apps that offer a range of portfolio options, including **index funds** and **ETFs**.
