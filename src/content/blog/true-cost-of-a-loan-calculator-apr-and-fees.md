## Introduction to True Cost of a Loan Calculator
The **true cost of a loan calculator** is a vital tool for borrowers to understand the actual cost of their loan, including **APR fees** and other charges. According to a [Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB)](https://www.consumerfinance.gov) report in 2020, lenders often use low APRs to lure borrowers in, but charge hidden fees that inflate the total cost. For instance, a 6% APR loan with a 5% origination fee can result in a significantly higher total cost.

## Understanding APR: The Misleading Interest Rate
Lenders often advertise low APRs to attract borrowers, but these rates do not always reflect the true cost of the loan. A study by the [Federal Reserve](https://www.federalreserve.gov) in 2022 found that APRs can be misleading, and borrowers should consider all fees associated with the loan. For example, a $10,000 loan with a 6% APR and a 5% origination fee would result in a total cost of $10,500.

## How Fees Add Up: Origination Fees, Late Payments, and More
Common loan fees, such as origination fees, late payment fees, and prepayment fees, can add up quickly and increase the total cost of the loan. According to data from the [Federal Reserve Economic Data](https://fred.stlouisfed.org), 2022, these fees can double or triple the original interest rate. Here is a comparison of common loan fees:
| Fee Type | Cost |
| --- | --- |
| Origination Fee | 1-5% of loan amount |
| Late Payment Fee | $25-$50 per late payment |
| Prepayment Fee | 1-3% of loan amount |

## The Power of Compounding: How Interest Accrues Over Time
Interest compounds over time, leading to exponential growth in costs. According to [Investopedia](https://www.investopedia.com), 2022, the power of compounding can result in a significant increase in the total cost of the loan. For example, a $10,000 loan with a 6% APR and a 5-year repayment term would result in a total cost of $13,180.
