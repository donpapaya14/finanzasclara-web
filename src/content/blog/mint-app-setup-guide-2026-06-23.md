## Introduction to Mint App
The **Mint budgeting app setup** is a crucial step in managing personal finances effectively. According to [Mint.com](https://www.mint.com), Mint offers a range of features, including bill tracking, investment monitoring, and budgeting tools. With over **20 million users**, Mint is one of the most popular personal finance management tools available. As stated on the [Mint.com official website (2022)](https://www.mint.com), Mint's benefits include **free credit scores**, **bill tracking**, and **investment monitoring**.

## Downloading and Installing Mint
To get started with Mint, follow these steps:
1. Download the Mint app from the App Store or Google Play Store.
2. Install the app on your mobile or desktop device.
3. Create a Mint account or log in if you already have one.
As per the [Mint Support Center (2023)](https://www.mint.com/support), Mint is compatible with **iOS 14.0 or later** and **Android 8.0 or later**.

## Configuring Mint for Budgeting
To configure Mint for budgeting, follow these steps:
1. Link your **bank accounts**, **credit cards**, and **investments** to track your spending.
2. Set up **budget categories** to categorize your expenses.
3. Monitor your **account activity** to stay on top of your finances.
According to a [NerdWallet review of Mint (2022)](https://www.nerdwallet.com), Mint's budgeting tools help users **save an average of $1,000 per year**.

## Setting Budgeting Goals and Alerts
To set budgeting goals and alerts, follow these steps:
1. Set **financial goals**, such as saving for a emergency fund or paying off debt.

### How do I link my bank accounts to Mint?
To link your bank accounts to Mint, follow these steps:
1. Log in to your Mint account.
2. Click on **'Add Account'**.
3. Select your bank from the list of available banks.
