## Introduction to Compound Interest
Compound interest is a powerful force in **long term investing**, allowing investors to grow their wealth over time. According to [Investopedia](https://www.investopedia.com), compound interest is the interest calculated on the initial principal, which also includes all the accumulated interest from previous periods. For example, if you invest $1,000 at a **5% interest rate** over 10 years, you can earn a total of $1,628.89, with $628.89 in interest earned. This is because the interest is compounded annually, meaning that the interest is added to the principal at the end of each year, and then the interest is calculated on the new principal balance.

## Compound Interest Formula and Calculation
The formula for calculating compound interest is A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt), where A is the amount of money accumulated after n years, including interest, P is the principal amount, r is the annual **interest rate**, n is the number of times that interest is compounded per year, and t is the time the money is invested for in years. According to [The Balance](https://www.thebalance.com), this formula can be used to calculate the future value of an investment. For example, if you invest $5,000 at a **7% interest rate** over 20 years, with interest compounded annually, you can earn a total of $20,595.85, with $15,595.85 in interest earned.
