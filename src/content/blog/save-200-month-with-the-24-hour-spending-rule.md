## Introduction to the 24-hour Spending Rule
The **24-hour spending rule** is a simple yet effective savings hack that can help you **reduce impulse buying** and save up to $200/month. By waiting 24 hours before buying non-essential items, you can **delayed spending** and make more informed purchasing decisions. According to a study by the [MIT Sloan School of Management](https://mitsloan.mit.edu/), 2022 impulse buying study, delaying purchases by 24 hours can lead to **30% fewer purchases**.

## What the 24-hour Rule is (and Why it Works)
The 24-hour spending rule is based on the idea of waiting 24 hours before buying non-essential items. This allows you to assess whether the item is something you really need or just a **impulse buy**. For example, if you see a $50 takeout meal that you want to buy on impulse, waiting 24 hours can help you realize that you don't need it, resulting in a **$50 savings**. Over time, this can add up to **$150/month** in savings.

## Step-by-Step: How to Implement it Today
To implement the 24-hour spending rule, follow these steps:
1. Set a dollar threshold (e.g., $25+) for items that require a 24-hour waiting period.
2. Bookmark items or screenshot ads instead of buying them immediately.
3. Use a notes app to log your 'want vs need' reasoning for each item.
According to the [National Endowment for Financial Education](https://www.nefe.org/), 2023, this simple hack can help you save up to **$960/year** by avoiding impulse purchases.

## The 3 Exceptions to the Rule
While the 24-hour spending rule is a great way to save money, there are some exceptions to the rule:
1. **True emergencies** (e.g., car repair)
2. **Limited-time necessities** (e.g., flight sale for a known trip)
3. **Pre-planned purchases** (with a budget line item)
According to the [Consumer Financial Protection Bureau](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/) guidelines, it's essential to make exceptions for these situations to avoid financial hardship.
