## Connecting Bank Accounts and Credit Cards
To get started with **Mint**, users need to connect their bank accounts, credit cards, and other financial institutions. Here are the step-by-step instructions:
1. Download and install the **Mint Mobile App (free download)**
2. Create an account or log in if you already have one
3. Click on 'Add Account' and select your bank or financial institution
4. Enter your login credentials to link your account
As per [Mint's Official Support Documentation](https://www.mint.com), this process is secure and encrypted to protect user data.

## Categorizing Transactions and Setting Budgets
Accurate categorization of transactions is crucial for effective budgeting. **Dave Ramsey's Budgeting Guide** recommends the 50/30/20 rule: 50% for necessities, 30% for discretionary spending, and 20% for saving and debt repayment. To set budgets in **Mint**:
1. Go to the 'Budget' tab
2. Select a category (e.g., housing, transportation)
3. Set a budget amount based on your income and expenses

## Using Mint's Budgeting Tools and Alerts
**Mint** offers various budgeting tools and alerts to help users stay on track. For example, the 'Bill Tracker' feature sends reminders for upcoming bills, and the 'Budgeting Alerts' feature notifies users when they exceed their budget. As explained in [Mint's Official Blog](https://blog.mint.com), these features can be customized to fit individual needs.
