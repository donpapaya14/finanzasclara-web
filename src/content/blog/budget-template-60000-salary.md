## Introduction to Budgeting on $60,000
Creating a **monthly budget template** for a **$60,000 salary** is crucial for managing finances effectively. According to the [National Foundation for Credit Counseling](https://www.nfcc.org/), 2022 data shows that 64% of Americans cannot cover a $1,000 emergency expense. The **50/30/20 rule** suggests allocating 50% of income towards fixed expenses, 30% towards discretionary spending, and 20% towards savings and debt repayment.

## Fixed Expenses Calculation
To calculate fixed expenses, consider **rent**, **utilities**, and **transportation costs**. Based on [Bureau of Labor Statistics](https://www.bls.gov/) 2023 data, the average American spends around 30% of their income on housing. A step-by-step guide to calculating fixed expenses:
1. Determine monthly income: $60,000 / 12 = $5,000
2. Allocate 50% towards fixed expenses: $5,000 * 0.5 = $2,500
3. Break down fixed expenses:
   * Rent: $1,500
   * Utilities: $150
   * Transportation: $500
   * Insurance: $200
   * Minimum debt payments: $100

## Discretionary Spending Allocation
For discretionary spending, allocate 30% of income towards **entertainment**, **hobbies**, and **travel**. The [American Savings Education Council](https://www.asec.org/) 2022 survey found that 61% of Americans spend more than they earn. Practical advice:
1. Identify discretionary income: $5,000 * 0.3 = $1,500
2. Allocate towards categories:
   * Entertainment: $500
   * Hobbies: $300
   * Travel: $700

## Savings and Debt Repayment Strategy
Setting **savings goals** and creating an **emergency fund** is essential. The [Financial Industry Regulatory Authority](https://www.finra.org/) 2023 report states that 40% of Americans have less than $1,000 in savings. Information on debt repayment:
1. Allocate 20% of income towards savings and debt repayment: $5,000 * 0.2 = $1,000
2. Prioritize high-interest debt: focus on paying off debts with interest rates above 6%
