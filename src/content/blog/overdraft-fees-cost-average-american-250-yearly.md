## Banks' Hidden Overdraft Triggers
There are several lesser-known transactions that can cause overdrafts, including pre-authorization holds, recurring payments processing early, and ATM withdrawals with pending deposits. The [Consumer Bankers Association](https://www.consumerbankersassociation.com/) 2024 guidelines highlight these hidden triggers, emphasizing the importance of understanding bank policies to **avoid overdraft fees**.

## Opt-out vs. Protection: Key Differences
When deciding how to manage overdrafts, it's crucial to understand the key differences between opting out completely and linking to savings. The [FDIC](https://www.fdic.gov/) overdraft protection rules provide guidelines on these options. The following flowchart can help decide which path to take:
| Option | Description | Fee Comparison |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Opt-out | Transactions decline | No fee |
| Link to Savings | Transfers from savings to cover overdrafts | Transfer fee may apply |
